STEP 9 (Optional) Create a user account. What is a Concept?

A concept is the core data manipulated by Knowledge.Bio to represent ideas about phenomena in the world. Most of the concepts in the system are related to biomedical subjects - for example, diseases, genes, and drugs - although the system relates these concepts to others such as people, methods, processes, etc.

 Are there any filters to refine the search results?

Yes! There are two options. 1. The filter by drop-down menu which allows you to filter concepts based on their types. 2. The text field to the right of the drop-down menu (Filter Concepts, Filter Relation Fields). It allows you to type keywords and then filter the result based on matching words.

 WHAT IS IT?

The 3 tabs on the left side of the Knowledge.Bio desktop - the Relations, Evidence, and Reference tabs - provide you ways to find concepts, link them to their evidence, and allow you to directly access the associated pubmed articles. The right side of the desktop displays a graphical representation of the concepts and relations of personal interest that you collect from the Relations table (see Concept Map FAQ).

 RELATIONS TAB What are the Subject and Object columns?

Subject and object refer to the concepts that are connected to each other. Their relationship is shown in the relation column.

 What is the Relation column?

The relation column represents how the subject and objects are connected using defined semantic relationships such as the Semantic Medline Database Ontological Predicate Definitions or perhaps WikiData Properties.

 What is the Evidence column for?

To access the articles related to the chosen concept. Simply click on the green buttons under the Evidence column.

The table most likely has been clipped to fit your screen resolution which causes parts of it to be hidden. A horizontal scrollbar should appear automatically to allow you to access the rest of the table.

How do I find more information about the subject or object?

When clicking on the subject or object in the table, it will bring up a pop-up window that shows basic information such as its definition, name, type, and Concept Universal Identifier (CUI). CUI is the identifier assigned to a concept by an authority that was the source of the definition. The pop-up window also gives the option to add the concept to the map as well as showing the related articles.

 How do I add or delete nodes on the map?

To add nodes, click on the check boxes in the table, and then click the Add to Map button. Alternatively, you can add a node to the map by clicking on any of the concepts under the Subject or Object column to open up a pop-up window with an Add to Map button as well.

To delete nodes, just select it on the map and a pop-up window should appear, giving you the option to delete the node.

 How to find related terms connected to a concept?

Click on your chosen subject or object to bring up the pop-up window. Click the Show Relations button.

 How do I find articles related to my searched concept?

Click the green buttons under the Evidence column. It will take you to the Evidence tab which lists all the related article links in the table. Click on any of them will take you to the Reference tab where you will be able to view the article.

 EVIDENCE TAB There is nothing under the Evidence tab!

In order to see the evidence for a relation, click the button in the Evidence column next to it in the Relations tab.

 Where do these articles come from and how are they chosen?

Much of the evidence is searched among the PubMed articles using text-mining technologies to find abstracts that have the mentioned concepts.

 REFERENCE TAB How do I access the articles?

The Evidence tab contains links to all the articles related to your chosen concept. Clicking on one of the sentences under the Supporting Text column will open the article in the Reference tab.

Can l read the article in a separate window?

Yes! Clicking on the Show Abstract in New Window button located above the article to make it easier to read.

 WHAT IS IT?

The map is used as a visual representation for the relationships between subjects and objects. It does this by adding nodes to the map to represent the subjects and objects, and edges (the arrows) to represent their relationships.

 NAVIGATION How do I zoom in and out of the map?

Use the scroll wheel or to zoom in and out of your map.

 How do I move the map?

Click and drag on a blank space to move the map around.

 How do I change the position of the nodes?

Click and drag on the specific nodes to move them.

 How can I find out more about a node?

Clicking on a node will bring up a pop-up window that shows its basic information (such as its name) as well as the option to show its relations in the Relations tab. The pop-up also allows you to delete the node from the map.

 MANAGING NODES AND EDGES How do I remove an element from the graph?

When you click on a node in the graph, a popup menu appears. Here there are actions that you can take with respect to the node. Click on Delete to remove the node and all its connected relations from the graph. You can still put them back by searching for the concept again and using the Add to Map button.

 Where can I find the properties of a particular concept I have on the graph?

Click on the node to produce a popup menu for the concept. Press Show Relationships. The Relationship tab will open. Clicking on the name of a concept in this tab will cause a pop-up to appear showing the properties for that concept.

 I have loaded/created a map and I want to see the evidence for a relation without having to look up the concept directly. How can I do this?

Every edge displayed on the map is clickable, producing a popup menu. For edges there will be a Show Evidence button. Clicking on it will bring you to the "Evidence" table, showing the sentences giving evidence to the relationship.

 How do I save the map and how do I access it?

Click on to save the map.

You can save your concept map in several formats: kb, sif, jpeg, or tsv. Each format has a purpose:

  • .kb - a file format specific to Knowledge.Bio that records all the content and layout details about a map.

  • .sif - the standard Cytoscape file format.

  • .jpeg - an image file format.

  • .tsv - a tab delimited text file format similar to .sif, except with the addition of unique concept identifiers.

 USER ACCOUNTS Why do I need an account?

A user account in Knowledge.Bio lets you save concept maps you create under your name. Additionally, you may have your own collection of private maps that you can selectively share with others.

 How do I create an account?

Click on the Login button in the sidebar. There will be a link, Sign Up. Click on it and follow the instructions.

 How do I login?

Click on the Login button in the sidebar and follow the instructions.

 How do I logout?

If you are logged in, the place where the Login button was previously will now state Logout. Click on the Logout button to sign out of the application and be returned to the initial page.

 Forgot your password?

Click on the Login button. On the login screen there will be a link, "Forgot your password?". Click on it and follow the options for recovery.
